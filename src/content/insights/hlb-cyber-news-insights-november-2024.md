**2\. New Cybersecurity Laws Could Reveal Hidden Risks**

Ireland is introducing new laws under the EU’s NIS2 Directive, designed to tighten cybersecurity rules and make businesses more accountable. The regulations will require organisations in key industries like healthcare, finance, and tech to report breaches more transparently. Experts predict this could double the number of reported breaches, uncovering vulnerabilities many companies may not have known about. While the directive aims to strengthen overall security, some businesses may struggle to meet the new requirements without expert support.

**Read the full story here**: [Irish Times Article on NIS2 Directive](https://www.irishtimes.com/technology/data-security/)

**3\. SETU Waterford Hit by Cyber Attack**

South East Technological University (SETU) in Waterford recently suffered a serious cyber attack, forcing the cancellation of classes. While investigations are ongoing, this incident highlights how academic institutions are becoming prime targets for hackers due to the large volumes of sensitive data they hold. This is a wake-up call for all organisations to step up their cybersecurity defences and protect against increasingly sophisticated threats.

**Read more on RTE**: [SETU Waterford Cyberattack Coverage](https://www.rte.ie/news/education/2024/1101/1478655-waterford-hack/)

**4\. Election Day Cyber Attacks: A Warning for Ireland**

In the United States, a series of DDoS attacks targeted political and election-related websites on Election Day. While these attacks were mitigated, they serve as a reminder of the growing threat of cyber incidents worldwide.
