FutureRange, in partnership with Mentora Group, today publishes the Irish SME Technology Report 2026 — a detailed look at how owner-managed businesses across Ireland are navigating the rapidly evolving technology landscape.

The report surveyed 340 Irish SMEs with between 10 and 250 employees, drawn from across professional services, retail, hospitality and construction.

## Key findings

- 67% of Irish SMEs have adopted some form of AI tool in the past 12 months, but only 23% have a formal policy governing its use
- Cybersecurity remains the single biggest technology concern, cited by 71% of respondents
- Only 34% of businesses have completed a formal cyber risk assessment in the last two years
- Cloud migration is accelerating — 58% of respondents have moved their primary business systems to cloud-based platforms, up from 39% in 2024

## The AI adoption gap

While adoption of AI tools is accelerating, there is a significant gap between tool adoption and strategic implementation. Many businesses are using AI for productivity tasks — drafting emails, summarising documents, generating content — without a clear understanding of the data risks involved.

> "The speed of AI adoption has outpaced governance. That creates real risk, particularly around data handling and client confidentiality. Businesses need a policy framework before they add more tools."
>
> — Eoin Kiersey, Chief Technology Officer, Mentora Group

## Cybersecurity: awareness vs action

Despite high awareness of cyber risk, the report reveals a significant gap between what businesses believe about their security posture and their actual exposure. The most common vulnerabilities identified were:

- Outdated multi-factor authentication practices
- Insufficient endpoint protection on remote devices
- Lack of tested incident response plans

The full report includes a practical self-assessment checklist that businesses can use to benchmark their current posture against industry standards.

## Sector breakdown

The report includes detailed breakdowns across four sectors — professional services, retail, hospitality and construction — revealing significant variation in technology maturity and investment priorities.

Professional services firms showed the highest rates of cloud adoption and AI tool usage, while construction and hospitality lagged on both metrics but showed greater awareness of cyber risk following high-profile incidents in their sectors.
