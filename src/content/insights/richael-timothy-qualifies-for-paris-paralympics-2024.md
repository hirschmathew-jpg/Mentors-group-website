*Originally published by [HLB Ireland](https://www.hlb.ie/richael-timothy-qualifies-for-paris-paralympics-2024/).*

After a life-altering injury, Richael transitioned from football to Paralympic cycling. "After my injury, I found a new direction in Paralympic cycling. Each competition represents a journey of resilience and determination," she said. HLB Ireland proudly sponsors Richael.

Managing Partner of HLB Ireland, Mark Butler, congratulated Richael, saying, "We are incredibly proud to support Richael on her journey to the Paris Paralympics. Her unwavering commitment to her sport, despite her challenges, is truly inspiring. Richael embodies the spirit of resilience and excellence, and we have seen firsthand how her dedication motivates those around her. At HLB Ireland, we believe in supporting individuals who strive for greatness, and Richael’s achievements exemplify the very best of what can be achieved with hard work and determination. We are honoured to stand behind her as she prepares to represent Ireland on the world stage."

Since joining the Paracycling squad in 2018, Richael has competed in the World Championships and the Tokyo 2020 Paralympics. Her Paris 2024 qualification highlights her dedication and hard work. She has followed an intense training regime to prepare, including camps with double gold medallist Mark Rohan, honing her skills on challenging terrains.

Beyond her athletic pursuits, Richael is deeply involved in her community. She runs a fitness business, organises classes for young children, and participates in the "Dare to Believe" program, inspiring schoolchildren with her Paralympic experiences. She also speaks on leadership, mental health, and LGBTQI+ inclusion in sports, reflecting her commitment to diversity and inclusion.

The Paris 2024 Paralympic Games will take place from 28 August to 8 September. HLB Ireland proudly supports her as she gears up to represent Ireland internationally.

##### [**About Richael Timothy:**](https://paralympics.ie/team-ireland-profiles/richael-timothy/) 

Richael Timothy is an Irish Paralympic cyclist from Ballymore, Co Roscommon, and currently training intensively for the upcoming Paris Paralympic Games. Formerly a soccer and Gaelic football player for her county, she pivoted to cycling following a career-ending medical condition. Since joining the para-cycling circuit, Richael has achieved international success, including podium finishes at world championships, demonstrating her extraordinary resilience and commitment.
