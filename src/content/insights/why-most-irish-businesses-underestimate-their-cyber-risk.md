There is a consistent pattern in how Irish businesses think about cybersecurity. They know it is important. They believe they have addressed it. And they are often wrong about the second part.

This is not a failure of intention — it is a failure of visibility. Most owner-managed businesses lack the internal resource to conduct a genuine risk assessment, and most of the tools designed to help them are either too technical to act on or too simplistic to be useful.

## The perception gap

FutureRange's latest research found that 71% of Irish SMEs consider cybersecurity a significant business risk. Yet only 34% had conducted a formal cyber risk assessment in the past two years.

More striking: of those who had conducted an assessment, 61% identified vulnerabilities they had not previously been aware of.

The conclusion is straightforward. Businesses consistently underestimate their actual exposure — not because they are careless, but because the threats are not visible in day-to-day operations.

## Where the risk actually lives

The most commonly underestimated risk areas identified in our research were not exotic attack vectors. They were mundane.

**Email compromise** remains the most common entry point for cybercriminals targeting Irish businesses. A convincing email — impersonating a supplier, a client or a senior executive — is still more effective than any piece of malware.

**Remote working infrastructure** introduced during the pandemic has in many cases never been properly secured. Personal devices accessing company systems, home routers with default credentials, and VPN configurations that have not been audited since 2020 remain common vulnerabilities.

**Third-party access** is frequently overlooked. If your business gives external accountants, IT contractors or software vendors access to your systems, each of those connections is a potential entry point.

## What to do

The good news is that the most impactful steps are not the most expensive ones. Based on our assessments across hundreds of Irish businesses, the following measures address the majority of common vulnerabilities:

- **Multi-factor authentication** on all business email accounts — this single step eliminates the vast majority of account compromise attacks
- **A documented and tested incident response plan** — most businesses have no plan for the first 24 hours of an attack
- **A formal third-party access review** — know who has access to your systems and why
- **An annual cyber risk assessment** — visibility is the prerequisite for everything else

## The role of managed IT

For businesses without a dedicated IT team, managed IT services offer a practical path to consistent security. A managed service provider monitors your environment continuously, applies patches before vulnerabilities are exploited, and provides a rapid response capability that would be prohibitively expensive to maintain in-house.
